**Novelty and Significance**

Antibiotic resistance represents a global health crisis, yet current microbiome-based alternatives face fundamental ecological barriers. Phage monotherapy triggers rapid bacterial defense systems and heteroresistance, leading to treatment failure through evolutionary arms races. Probiotics alone cannot overcome colonization resistance in established microbial communities.

Here we present a bioinformatics-driven platform that rationally predicts and validates phage-probiotic synergistic consortia from metagenomics data. Our key innovations are:

1. **Computational framework**: Integration of vOTU-host interaction networks, niche overlap analysis, and metabolic cross-feeding predictions to calculate synergy scores—the first systematic approach to rational phage-probiotic design.

2. **Mechanistic insights**: We demonstrate that rationally matched phages induce cascading effects that eliminate pathogens while releasing metabolic byproducts that facilitate probiotic engraftment, effectively short-circuiting the evolutionary arms race.

3. **Validation across scales**: From in silico predictions to in vitro validation and in vivo avian models, we show >2 log10 CFU/g pathogen reduction and stable probiotic colonization—outperforming monotherapies while preventing phage-resistant mutant emergence.

**Why Nature Communications**

This work aligns with *Nature Communications*' scope by providing a generalizable paradigm for rational microbiome engineering with broad applicability across agricultural, clinical, and environmental contexts. Our bioinformatics platform addresses a fundamental gap in microbiome therapeutics: the lack of predictive frameworks for multi-species consortia design. The mechanistic insights into niche clearing, metabolic cross-feeding, and immune modulation offer new conceptual advances in viral-bacterial interactions and microbial ecology.
